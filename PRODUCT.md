# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Trade fair operators, booth analytics staff, and technical coordinators using a desktop control station during setup and live sessions. They need to verify camera health, understand booth occupancy quickly, and react to calibration or sync issues without losing the spatial context of the venue.

## Product Purpose

Fair Monitor coordinates multiple camera feeds on top of a shared 2D world map to produce booth occupancy, visits, time spent, and overlap-aware counting. The product succeeds when an operator can switch attention between cameras instantly, trust the overlap-calibrated map view, and identify runtime issues before analytics quality degrades.

## Brand Personality

Focused, precise, composed. The interface should feel like a reliable control room tool: technically credible, calm under pressure, and direct about system state.

## Anti-references

Do not make this look like a generic purple SaaS dashboard, a CCTV wall with equal-weight tiles, or a consumer smart-home control app. Avoid glossy marketing visuals, decorative charts, and identity-centric surveillance styling.

## Design Principles

- Keep the operator anchored on the current booth situation, not on a wall of competing feeds.
- Make camera switching immediate, obvious, and low-friction.
- Tie the spatial map to the active camera so source context is never ambiguous.
- Expose runtime health continuously through calm signals instead of hidden diagnostics.
- Preserve calibration and zone editing clarity while the system is live.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for core controls and status text. Ensure keyboard-reachable primary actions, visible focus states, readable dense telemetry, and status cues that do not rely on color alone. Keep motion minimal and purposeful.
